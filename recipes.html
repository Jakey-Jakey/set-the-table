the cupcake showdown

red carpet chocolate cupcakes

inspired by Liam Hamilton's drama
⏱ 45 min 🍽 makes 12

cupcake batter

  • 1½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup cocoa powder
  • 1½ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ cup melted butter
  • 1 cup milk (any kind works!)
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ cup hot water or coffee

vanilla buttercream

  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • 4 cups icing sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla
  • 3 tbsp cream or milk
  • pinch of salt

topping

  • gold sprinkles
  • chocolate shavings
  • fresh raspberries

directions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F. Line a muffin tin with cupcake liners.
  2. Whisk flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt in a big bowl.
  3. In another bowl, mix together the sugar, eggs, melted butter, milk, and vanilla.
  4. Pour the wet stuff into the dry stuff and stir until just combined — don't overmix! Then stir in the hot water. The batter will be thin and that's totally fine.
  5. Fill your liners about ⅔ full. Bake for 18–20 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean. Let them cool completely before frosting.
  6. For the buttercream: Beat the softened butter by itself for about 3 minutes until it's fluffy and pale. Add the icing sugar one cup at a time, then the vanilla, cream, and salt. Beat on high for 2 minutes until it's smooth and light.
  7. Pipe or spread the buttercream onto your cooled cupcakes. Hit them with gold sprinkles, some chocolate shavings, and a raspberry on top.
💡 the hot water/coffee trick makes the chocolate flavour way deeper — seriously don't skip it, you can't taste the coffee!

strawberry shortcake cupcakes

inspired by Maddie's episode
⏱ 50 min 🍽 makes 12

cupcake batter

  • 1¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1½ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • ½ cup softened butter
  • 2 eggs
  • ¾ cup milk
  • 2 tsp vanilla
  • ½ cup freeze-dried strawberries, crushed fine

chocolate drizzle

  • 100g chocolate chips
  • ¼ cup cream

whipped cream top

  • 1½ cups heavy cream, cold
  • 3 tbsp icing sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • fresh strawberries

directions

  1. Preheat to 350°F. Line your muffin tin.
  2. Whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, and your crushed freeze-dried strawberry powder.
  3. In another bowl, beat the butter and sugar together until pale and fluffy (3–4 min with a hand mixer, longer by hand). Add eggs one at a time, then vanilla.
  4. Add the flour mixture and milk in 3 alternating additions, mixing gently between each one.
  5. Fill liners ⅔ full. Bake 20–22 min. Cool completely.
  6. Chocolate drizzle: Heat the cream until it just starts to simmer, pour it over the chocolate chips. Wait 2 minutes, then stir until smooth and glossy. Let it cool a bit.
  7. Whipped cream: Beat the cold cream with icing sugar and vanilla until you get stiff peaks.
  8. Drizzle chocolate over the cupcakes, pipe on the whipped cream, top with a sliced strawberry. Done!
💡 the freeze-dried strawberry powder gives the cake a natural pink colour and real berry flavour without making the batter wet

the smoky cream soda

inspired by Deevs' episode
⏱ 10 min 🍽 makes 2 drinks

ingredients

  • 1 can cream soda, chilled
  • 2 oz coconut cream
  • 1 oz fresh lime juice
  • ½ oz simple syrup
  • ¼ tsp liquid smoke (trust us)
  • a few dashes of sriracha
  • strawberry sparkling water
  • ice

garnish

  • Tajín rim
  • lime wheel

directions

  1. Run a lime wedge around the rim of two glasses, then dip them in Tajín. Fill with ice.
  2. In a jar or shaker, combine coconut cream, lime juice, simple syrup, liquid smoke, and sriracha. Shake it up for about 10 seconds to mix everything together.
  3. Pour the mixture evenly between your two glasses.
  4. Top each glass halfway with cream soda, then fill the rest with strawberry sparkling water.
  5. Give it one gentle stir. Add a lime wheel. You're done!
💡 the liquid smoke sounds wild but it adds a subtle depth that works surprisingly well with cream soda. start with less sriracha and add more to taste!

the cranberry citrus spritz

inspired by Maddie's hosting
⏱ 5 min 🍽 makes 2 drinks 📊 super easy

ingredients

  • 3 oz cranberry juice (100%, not cocktail)
  • 2 oz fresh lime juice
  • 1 oz coconut cream or piña colada mix
  • ½ oz simple syrup
  • grapefruit sparkling water
  • ginger ale
  • ice

garnish

  • grapefruit wedge
  • sprig of rosemary

directions

  1. Fill two tall glasses with ice.
  2. Split the cranberry juice, lime juice, coconut cream, and simple syrup between the glasses. Give each a stir.
  3. Top each glass with equal parts grapefruit sparkling water and ginger ale (about 3 oz of each).
  4. Stir gently. Tuck in a grapefruit wedge and a rosemary sprig. Enjoy!
💡 this one is genuinely great for impressing people. the cranberry-grapefruit combo gives it a gorgeous deep pink colour
