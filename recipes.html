episode 1 — red carpet craving

cookbook recipe

post-shoot blues

a cooling blueberry mocktail
episode guest: Liam Hamilton bright, bittersweet, and calming

about the drink

Liam Hamilton shared this bittersweet mocktail as a post-shoot cooldown. It starts bright and zesty, then settles into blueberry and lavender notes for that tired-but-satisfied feeling after a long day.

the recipe

  1. Simmer ½ cup sugar, ½ cup water, and 1 tbsp dried lavender; steep 10-15 minutes, strain, and cool.
  2. Muddle ½ cup blueberries with 1 oz lime juice.
  3. Add ¾ oz lavender syrup, 2 oz pineapple juice, 1 dash orange bitters (optional), and ice; shake well.
  4. Strain into a glass with ice, top with 3 oz sparkling water, and stir.
  5. Garnish and serve.
Enjoy your soothing and fruitful drink!

episode 2 — shake it to the beat!

cookbook recipe

feel the deeves

cheesy tuna melt sandwich
episode guest: Deevs a salty pre-show comfort pick

about the dish

Deevs says this cheesy tuna melt is the sandwich he eats before every concert. The canned tuna matches the salty attitude, the cheese matches the cheesy mood, and the whole thing sets the rhythm for the night.

the recipe

  1. Drain one 180 ml can of chunk light tuna. Flake with a fork and mix with 2 tbsp mayonnaise, 1 tbsp minced white onion, 1 tbsp finely minced celery, 1 tbsp sweet pickle relish, ½ tsp lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  2. Spread extra mayonnaise on one side of 4 slices of bread.
  3. Place 2 slices in a skillet (mayo side down). Top each with 1 slice of cheddar or marble cheese, half the tuna mix, another cheese slice, and the remaining bread (mayo side up).
  4. Cook on medium heat until the bottom is golden (3-4 minutes). Flip and cook until the other side is golden and the cheese melts.
  5. Slice in half and serve hot.
Enjoy your cheesy and resourceful snack!

episode 3 — superfood!

cookbook recipe

bat-treat

oatmeal Belgian waffles with kiwi and banana toppings
episode guest: Batman comfort food with a protein boost

about the dish

Batman shared the comfort meal Alfred makes when the vigilante needs a mental break. The waffles are sweet and satisfying, but still built around oatmeal, eggs, and kefir for a more filling reset.

the recipe

  1. Put 100 gr of oatmeal in a blender and turn it into flour.
  2. Add 2 whole eggs and two egg whites to the blend.
  3. Add 150 grams of kefir to the mixture.
  4. Mix everything in your blender one more time.
  5. Bake your waffles in a pre-heated waffle maker.
  6. While letting the waffles rest, slice a kiwi and a banana and add them on top of the waffles.
  7. If you feel like it, add a small amount of whipped cream and coconut flakes to make the treat even sweeter.
Enjoy your fluffy and delicious protein packed desert!

episode 4 — friend or foe?!

cookbook recipe

sinking ship

tomato cream soup
episode guests: Dax and Tiffany rich soup with sinking croutons

about the dish

Dax and Tiffany shared their favorite rich tomato soup. The cookbook frames it as a dramatic bowl that starts steady, then sinks under the weight of cream and croutons.

the recipe

  1. In a pot over medium heat, saute 1 small minced onion and 2 minced garlic cloves in 1 tbsp oil for 2 minutes.
  2. Add 1 can (398 ml) diced tomatoes, 1 cup vegetable or chicken broth, 1 tsp dried basil, ½ tsp smoked paprika, ½ tsp salt, and ¼ tsp black pepper. Simmer 10 minutes.
  3. Stir in ½ cup heavy cream (or coconut milk) and heat 2 more minutes until the soup turns darker and richer.
  4. While the soup simmers, toss 1 cup of cubed bread with 1 tbsp melted butter and 1 tbsp grated parmesan. Bake or air-fry at 200°C (400°F) for 5 minutes until crispy.
  5. Ladle the soup into bowls. Drop the crispy croutons on top so they slowly sink into the dark broth. Finish with an extra swirl of cream on top.
Enjoy your creamy and spicy passionate soup (before the divorce)!

episode 5 — roast dinner!

cookbook recipe

nacho business

taco bake casserole
episode guest: Kendall Barnes a guilty-pleasure TV snack

about the dish

Kendall Barnes shared her guilty pleasure snack for unwinding after a successful stand-up set. The cookbook page focuses on the layered bake method rather than a full ingredient card, so the site now mirrors that version directly.

the recipe

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F and coat a medium casserole dish or 9x13 baking dish with cooking spray. Set aside.
  2. In a large cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat, brown the ground beef.
  3. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Cook until the onion softens.
  4. Stir in the diced tomatoes with green chiles, beef broth, cilantro, and taco seasoning. Cook for about 3 minutes.
  5. Add the sour cream and ½ cup of the shredded cheese. Mix well, then remove from heat and let the mixture thicken slightly.
  6. Coarsely crumble half of the tortilla chips and spread them in the bottom of the casserole dish.
  7. Layer half of the beef mixture and then half of the remaining shredded cheese on top of that.
  8. Repeat the layers once more.
  9. Bake uncovered for 20 minutes.
  10. Let cool for 5 minutes, then serve with your desired toppings.
Enjoy your crispy and savoury snack!
